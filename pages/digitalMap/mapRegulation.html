Elements of Maps

Some common features of maps include title, map scale, map legend, orientation, and grids. Other features that appear regularly are the date, author, index, and sources.

5 Elements of Maps:

Title

The title indicates the theme of maps, explaining what is represented in the image.

Map Scale

All maps are scale models of reality. A map’s scale indicates the relationship between the distances on the map and the actual distances on Earth. The most common type of graphic scale looks like a ruler also called a bar scale. It is simply a horizontal line marked off in miles, kilometers, or other measuring distance. A map that shows an area in great detail, such as a street map of a neighborhood, is called a large-scale map because objects on the map are relatively large. A map of a larger area, such as a continent or the world, is called a small-scale map because objects on the map are relatively small.

Map Legend

A map's legend explains what the symbols on a map mean. It contains a list of different symbols and colors next to a brief explanation of each character's meaning.

Orientation

Orientation refers to the presence of a compass rose or simply an arrow indicating directions on the map. If only an arrow is used, the arrow usually points north. These directional identifiers enable maps to be drawn from any orientation with respect to the Earth’s surface without losing accuracy.

Grids

Many maps include a grid pattern or a series of crossing lines. The grid helps people locate places on the map. On small-scale maps, the grid often comprises latitude and longitude lines. On maps showing greater detail, the grid is often given numbers and letters. The boxes made by the grid are called A, B, C, and so on across the top of the map, and 1, 2, 3, and so on across the left side.

Other Elements of Maps：

Date

“Date” refers to either the time maps were made or the date relevant to the information on maps. A map of areas threatened by a wildfire, for instance, would have a date or a time to track the progress of the wildfire.

Author

A map's author is essential because the perspective of the cartographer will be reflected in the content.

Index

A map’s index helps viewers find a specific spot on the map using the grid.

Sources

A map's sources are where the map's author gets their information. A school district map may list the global positioning system (GPS) technology and the school district's records as its sources.
