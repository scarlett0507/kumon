About Tsunami

Tsunamis are giant waves caused by earthquakes or volcanic eruptions under the sea. The waves travel inland. They build up to higher and higher heights as the depth of the ocean decreases. The speed of tsunami waves depends on ocean depth rather than the distance from the source of the wave.

Causes

The principal generation mechanism of a tsunami is the displacement of a substantial volume of water or perturbation of the sea. This water displacement is usually attributed to earthquakes, landslides, volcanic eruptions, or glacier calvings.

Historically, the Nankai Trench earthquake has caused significant damage to Mie Prefecture in Japan about every 100-150 years in the past. Japan created the Mie Prefecture tsunami hazard map to prepare for disaster in advance. Therefore, the Web hazard map of Matsuzaka City is taken as an example to understand the scope of the tsunami.
